The Problem

AI gives people time back.
Most waste it on more work.

Everyone is talking about AI saving time. But the saved hours go right back into the grind. Not into family. Not into faith. Not into the thing they were put on this earth to do. The technology is powerful. The intention is missing.

The Trap

AI makes you faster. So you do more. More meetings, more emails, more projects. The hamster wheel spins faster but you never step off it.

The Cost

You are at your desk when you should be at dinner. On your phone when your children are in the room. Present at work and absent at home.

The Founders

Mission-driven people with great ideas but no technical ability to build them. They are stuck at the idea stage because they cannot ship the product themselves.

The Answer

Technology built with intention. AI that points people back toward their vocation. Partnerships that turn trust and skill into companies that serve people.

The Filter

Trust first. Idea second.
Money last.

Forge does not partner with everyone. There are four criteria. All four must be true.

Deep Trust

Do I trust this person with my name? Would I go to war with them? This is non-negotiable. No exceptions. I will turn down a deal if the trust is not there.

Belief in the Idea

Does this solve a real problem? Is there a clear path to revenue? I do not build vanity projects. The idea has to matter.

Domain Expertise

Does the founder bring something I cannot build myself? Industry knowledge, relationships, and credibility in their space. The partnership has to be complementary.

Faith Alignment

Does this work serve a purpose beyond money? Does it protect vocations, serve people, or advance the mission? If the answer is no, we do not build it.

The Calling

I grew up without a father.
That is why I do this.

I know what it feels like when a father is not present. I lived it. I grew up without mine. That absence shaped everything about who I am and what I am building.

One of my biggest ambitions in life is to become a father. It is the thing I am orienting my entire life around. Learning to love well. Learning to lead well. Learning to be the man my future family will need me to be.

I had a business that was working. It was growing, earning, building credibility. But it was consuming me. I was putting God aside. And when I do that, it weighs on me heavily and eventually comes crashing down. So I walked away. Not because the work was bad. Because it was pulling me from what matters most.

That is why I serve Catholic fathers and mission-driven founders. I see men who love their families, who take their vocation seriously, but who are falling into the same trap: getting so lost in their work that they are absent from the people God gave them.

Forge exists to fix that. Every system I build, every company I accelerate, every hour I recover for a leader is one more hour they get to be present. That is the only metric that matters.
