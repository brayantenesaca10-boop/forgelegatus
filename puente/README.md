# Puente - Connection Beyond Language

## What Is This?

An NFC ring + mobile app that bridges language and culture in multicultural communities. Starting in Danbury, CT. Not a business card - an identity and connection layer.

**Name: Puente** - Spanish for "Bridge." Luxury feel, cultural roots, meaningful. The name IS the product - bridging people across language and culture. Works in English and Spanish. Feels elevated, not techy.

**Tagline:** "Connection beyond language"

---

## Brand Identity

### Name: Puente
- Pronounced: "PWEN-teh"
- Meaning: Bridge (Spanish)
- Why it works: luxury brands use foreign words (Hermès, Porsche, Moët). A Spanish name for a product built for multicultural communities is authentic, not performative.

### Logo Concept
- The word "Puente" with the "u" and "e" connected by a subtle arc/bridge line
- Or a minimal bridge icon - two dots connected by an arc (represents two people bridged)
- Clean, one color, works embossed on packaging, engraved on ring, clean on app

### Color Palette
- Primary: Matte black + warm white
- Accent: Gold or terracotta (warm, cultural, luxury - NOT tech blue)
- This says luxury + warmth. Not Silicon Valley.

### Brand Language
- Never say "networking." Say: connect, bridge, find your people, tell your story
- Never say "digital business card." Say: your story, your voice, your people
- Ping is a tool. Puente is an experience.

### The Sound
- Custom notification sound when someone connects with you
- Warm, resonant - like a soft chime or wooden knock
- NOT a generic ding. This is the sonic brand.
- Commission from Fiverr sound designer ($30-50)

### Packaging (The Luxury Experience)
- Magnetic closure box, matte black, soft-touch exterior
- "Puente" logo debossed on top (no ink, just pressed into the material)
- Inside: ring on a small cushion/foam insert
- One card underneath: thick stock, QR code + "Tap your ring. Tell your story."
- Round sticker seal with logo on box closure
- Cost: $2-3/unit from Alibaba (search "magnetic jewelry box custom logo")
- This turns a $29 purchase into a $100 feeling

### Ring Design
- Zirconia ceramic (ZrO2), matte black
- Thin band (4-6mm) - looks like jewelry, not a gadget
- Clean exterior - no logos, no text, no branding on the outside
- "Puente" engraved on inner band (hidden detail, luxury touch)
- Beveled edge on one side to catch light and feel designed

## Why This Exists

Places like Danbury are rich with people from different cultures and languages - Dominican, Mexican, Ecuadorian, Brazilian, American - but the tools for connection are built for English-speaking tech workers. No one is building for multicultural communities where language barriers cost people jobs, connections, and opportunities daily.

Ping (getping.today) sells NFC rings that share a digital profile. It's shallow: English-only, text-only, no app, no story, no warmth, no relationship layer. Tap and forget.

We go deeper.

## Core Features

### 1. Bilingual Story Profiles (Voice-First)
- Tap the ring, get a rich profile with your story told in YOUR voice
- AI voice cloning in both English and Spanish (or Portuguese, Creole, etc.)
- Listener picks their language, hears YOUR voice telling YOUR story in THEIR language
- Voice-first input: record your profile by talking, AI transcribes and translates
- Inspired by how Gary Sheng tells his story at garysheng.com/story - but with voice and multilingual

### 2. Interest Graph + Visual Connections
- Add your interests to your profile (soccer, cooking, faith, carpentry, music, etc.)
- When you connect with someone, you visually see what connects you
- Lines drawn between shared interests - like a relationship map
- Example: I like soccer, I connect with a soccer coach, I see that connection via a line between us
- Makes the invisible visible - you instantly see WHY you should talk to this person

### 3. Conversation Starters
- Based on shared interests, the app generates conversation starters
- "You both played soccer growing up - ask about their favorite team"
- "You're both from Guayaquil - ask what neighborhood"
- Removes the awkwardness, especially across language barriers

### 4. The App (Ping Doesn't Have One)
- Native mobile app (React Native)
- Every new connection triggers the Puente sound notification
- See your connection history, your interest graph, your community
- Ping only has a web profile - no app, no notifications, no engagement loop

### 5. Context-Aware Connections (Puente Engine)
- When two people tap, the app shows what they have in common
- Shared hometown region, kids at the same school, same church, complementary skills
- We don't just dump a profile - we build the BRIDGE

### 6. Community Discovery Layer
- "Who else in Danbury is from Guayaquil?"
- "Who speaks Portuguese and does plumbing?"
- Local directory that grows organically through taps
- Churches, community centers, and local businesses become hubs

### 7. Trust Signals
- Endorsements from community members, church leaders, employers
- "Maria vouches for this person" carries real weight
- Social proof built the way these communities actually build trust - through relationships

# LAUNCH PLAN (Rings in Hand by May 7)

## Phase 0: Legal Foundation (April 11-25, runs in parallel) - $500-800

### Trademark
- Run a formal TESS search on USPTO.gov for "Puente" in Classes 9, 35, 38, and 42
- "Puente" is less saturated than "Tappy" in tech classes - likely clear
- Budget $300-500 for a trademark attorney knockout search
- File a trademark application once clear ($250-350 filing fee)

### IP Protection - Avoiding Ping Disputes
- **Patent risk: LOW.** The tap-to-open-URL mechanic is standard NFC Forum spec (NDEF records launching a URL). Open standard - not patentable by any single company
- Ping, Popl, Dot - none have meaningful patent moats. They rely on trade dress and branding
- **What we MUST do:**
  - Do NOT copy Ping's UI, profile layout, or visual design - differentiate everything
  - Do NOT use their marketing language or taglines
  - Our value prop is fundamentally different: multilingual, voice, interest graph, community - not a digital business card
  - Document our independent creation process (this repo serves as evidence)
- **NFC hardware:** Off-the-shelf NTAG216 inlays - commodity chips, no patent exposure
- **FCC compliance:** Passive NFC tags are generally exempt from FCC Part 15 certification, but verify

### Business Formation
- Register LLC in Connecticut ($120)
- Get EIN from IRS (free)
- Open business bank account
- Basic terms of service + privacy policy (template, $0-200 for legal review)
- Privacy policy MUST cover: voice data collection, CCPA compliance, contact data handling
